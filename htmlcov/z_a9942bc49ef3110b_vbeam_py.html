1"""Resonant V-beam: two long wires splayed into a V (L. B. Cebik, W4RNL). 

2 

3A single long wire (1 wavelength or more) is not broadside like a dipole -- its 

4main lobes swing toward the wire's own axis, making a shallow cone a fixed 

5angle off the wire. Put two such long wires together at an apex, opening them by 

6twice that angle, and the forward lobes of the two legs line up along the 

7bisector and ADD: a bidirectional beam off both ends of the V's centreline, 

8HORIZONTALLY POLARISED, with more gain than a dipole for the wire used. It is 

9the standing-wave (resonant, un-terminated) sibling of the terminated rhombic 

10-- which is just two V-beams placed nose to nose with a load. 

11 

12This fills the "resonant long-wire array" gap directly alongside the existing 

13terminated `rhombic`: same wire family, but bidirectional (no termination, no 

14DC path to burn the back lobe). 

15 

16Cebik / long-wire theory: for ~1 wl legs the lobe sits ~36 degrees off each 

17wire, so the included apex angle is ~2 * 36 = ~72 degrees (each leg ~36 degrees 

18off the bisector). Longer legs want a narrower apex and give more gain. 

19 

20Geometry, in the framework's (x, y, z) convention: 

21 - x : the bisector / firing axis; the apex sits at -x, the legs open toward 

22 +x, and the beam fires bidirectionally off +/- x 

23 - y : the two legs splay symmetrically to +/- y 

24 - z : constant height `base` (the V lies in a horizontal plane) 

25 

26 o (leg end, +y) 

27 / 

28 F===< apex feed at -x, opening toward +x 

29 \\ 

30 o (leg end, -y) beam <--> +/- x (along the bisector) 

31""" 

32 

33from ... import AntennaBuilder 

34from types import MappingProxyType 

35import math 

36 

37 

38class Builder(AntennaBuilder): 

39 default_params = MappingProxyType( 

40 { 

41 "design_freq": 28.57, 

42 "freq": 28.57, 

43 "base": 7.0, 

44 # Leg length as a fraction of a wavelength (each wire). Longer -> 

45 # more gain, narrower apex. 

46 "leg_frac": 1.0, 

47 # Half the included apex angle, in degrees: each leg sits this far 

48 # off the bisector. ~36 deg suits ~1 wl legs. 

49 "half_apex_deg": 36.0, 

50 "ui_params": MappingProxyType( 

51 { 

52 # Long-wire apex feed is high and reactive; open-wire fed. 

53 "target_z0": 300.0, 

54 # Legs splay in x/y -> the xy view is face-on. 

55 "default_view": "xy", 

56 "leg_frac": { 

57 "min": 0.75, 

58 "max": 2.0, 

59 }, 

60 "half_apex_deg": { 

61 "min": 20.0, 

62 "max": 55.0, 

63 "step": 0.1, 

64 "precision": 2, 

65 }, 

66 } 

67 ), 

68 } 

69 )

71 def build_wires(self): 

72 eps = 0.05 

73 wavelength = 299.792458 / self.design_freq 

74 quarter = 0.25 * wavelength 

75 

76 leg = self.leg_frac * wavelength 

77 theta = math.radians(self.half_apex_deg) 

78 dx = math.cos(theta) 

79 dy = math.sin(theta) 

80 z = self.base 

81 

82 # Apex near the origin, legs opening toward +x at +/- theta off the 

83 # bisector. A one-segment driven gap bridges the two inner ends. 

84 inner_p = (eps * dx, eps * dy, z) # toward +y leg 

85 inner_m = (eps * dx, -eps * dy, z) # toward -y leg 

86 end_p = (leg * dx, leg * dy, z) 

87 end_m = (leg * dx, -leg * dy, z) 

88 

89 arm = self.segs_for(leg - eps, quarter) 

90 

91 tups = [] 

92 # Driven gap across the apex between the two legs' inner ends. 

93 tups.append((inner_m, inner_p, 1, 1 + 0j)) 

94 tups.append((inner_p, end_p, arm, None)) # +y leg 

95 tups.append((inner_m, end_m, arm, None)) # -y leg 

96 return tups
