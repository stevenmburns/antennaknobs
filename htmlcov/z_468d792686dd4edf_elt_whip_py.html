1"""Parametric rebuild of the ELT whip on a 96-inch rounded ground-plane grid 

2— the heavier of the two classic NEC performance benchmarks 

3(``whip_antenna_8ft_groundplane.nec`` from the W8IO NEC benchmarks page, 

4http://www.w8io.com/nec-benchmarks.htm): 434 deck wires, ~4,400 segments. 

5It is in the catalog to stress the solvers, not to be a usable antenna — 

6expect seconds-per-solve knob response. 

7 

8Where the source deck hand-authors 434 GW cards, this design *generates* 

9the same geometry from knobs. At the default parameter values the wire 

10paths and segment boundaries reproduce the deck to well under 0.1 mm 

11(the only differences are ~1e-8 m, from the deck writing cos(30 deg) as the 

12truncated literal .216506). The structure: 

13 

14 * **Whip** — a thin lower section (fed on its bottom segment, exactly where 

15 the deck's EX card drives it) and a thicker upper section, whose segment 

16 count scales with ``nominal_nsegs`` via ``segs_for`` (28 at the default 

17 mesh, matching the deck; see ``_WHIP_UPPER_REF``). 

18 * **Cage/sleeve** — a ring of ``num_cage_wires`` verticals at 

19 ``cage_radius`` around the lower whip, tied by a polygon ring every 

20 ``cage_ring_spacing``, bonded to the whip by ``num_spokes`` spokes one 

21 ring-spacing up and to the ground mesh by ``num_posts`` grounded posts. 

22 * **Ground plane** — a wire grid at ``grid_pitch``, trimmed to a rounded 

23 outline of radius ``plane_radius``, with the central row/column and the 

24 centre 2x2 cells re-meshed at half the grid pitch, and the feed row split 

25 at the post feet (at ``cage_radius``-length segments, as in the deck). 

26 

27Three quantities the deck fixes are derived rather than exposed as knobs: 

28the cage's bottom-ring height (= ``cage_ring_spacing``), the fine-mesh pitch 

29(= ``grid_pitch`` / 2), and the upper-whip segment count (from 

30``nominal_nsegs``). Reparameterized 2026-07-20. 

31 

32Every mesh crossing is emitted as a wire *endpoint* junction (unit cell 

33edges), so the geometry survives the engines' odd-parity re-segmentation — 

34the same form a NEC deck import's junction-splitting pass would produce 

35(mid-wire junctions don't survive resegmentation; see the NEC-import docs). 

36 

37**The outline is data, not a formula.** The deck's "rounded" boundary is 

38hand-drawn: no circle radius reproduces its column extents (it isn't even 

39x/y symmetric — the node at (28", 40") is inside, (40", 28") is not). The 

40profile is therefore kept as a table of the deck's 25 column half-extents, 

41normalized, and resampled linearly when the knobs change the column count; 

42rows are derived as its exact transpose, as in the deck. At the default 24 

43cells it is used verbatim. 

44 

45Constraints worth knowing: 

46 * Keep ``num_cage_wires`` a multiple of 4 if you use posts/spokes on all 

47 four axes: posts bond to the ground mesh only where their feet land on 

48 the centre mesh lines (the +/-x and +/-y axes). An off-axis post is still 

49 drawn but its foot floats above the mesh. 

50 * ``num_posts``/``num_spokes`` pick evenly spaced cage wires starting at 

51 +x, so 2 posts land on +/-x (the deck) and 4 spokes on +/-x/+/-y (the 

52 deck's two crossed diameter bars). 

53 * The deck's LD matching network IS modelled (unlike a raw deck import, 

54 which records the LD cards in ``deck.ignored``): ``build_network`` 

55 puts ``post_l_nh`` (deck: 90 nH) in series with the first grounded 

56 post and ``post_c_pf`` (deck: 3 pF) in series with the second — the 

57 cage's two grounded legs are the matching network. Set a knob to 0 to 

58 omit that element. 

59 * The upper whip carries its own per-wire spec (issue #388) with the 

60 deck's true 0.035" = 0.889 mm radius (``whip_upper_radius``); everything 

61 else uses ``wire_radius`` (the deck's 0.254 mm). PyNEC honors both, and 

62 every momwire solver honors both since momwire#147 (complete in 

63 momwire 0.13.0) — the whip solves at its true radius. Measured 

64 2026-07-15 at 406 MHz, free space: momwire sinusoidal 63.09+8.09j 

65 vs PyNEC 63.02+8.06j through the matching network (0.072 Ω apart). 

66"""

74# Reference length for the thick upper whip's segment count. The deck meshes its 

75# 15.75" (0.40005 m) top section into 28 segments; dividing that length by the 

76# default nominal_nsegs (21) and multiplying back gives a fixed reference so 

77# ``segs_for(whip_upper_len, _WHIP_UPPER_REF)`` reproduces the deck's 28 segments 

78# at the default mesh and refines proportionally under a convergence sweep. It is 

79# a constant, NOT the live ``whip_upper_len`` — that is what lets ``segs_for`` 

80# track the wire's length rather than pin the count. 

81_WHIP_UPPER_REF = 0.40005 * 21 / 28 # ≈ 0.30004 m

85# outline; index 0 is the centre column, index 24 the rim.

115def _column_extent(i, n): 

116 """Half-extent, in cells, of column ``|i|`` on a plane ``n`` cells in 

117 radius: the deck profile resampled to ``n``. Exact for n == 24.""" 

118 m = len(_GRID_PROFILE) - 1 

119 x = abs(i) * m / n 

120 k = min(int(x), m - 1) 

121 e = _GRID_PROFILE[k] + (x - k) * (_GRID_PROFILE[k + 1] - _GRID_PROFILE[k]) 

122 return max(0, round(e * n / m))

184 # Coarser ground screen held just inside the λ/10 wire-grid-as-conductor 

185 # rule of thumb: 17 cells across the 48" plane → 71.7 mm pitch ≈ λ/10.3 at 

186 # 406 MHz (the default's 50.8 mm is λ/14.5; a full 2× coarsening would reach 

187 # λ/7.3 and detune the match). ~1.8× fewer segments (4392 → 2472) — and since 

188 # the dense MoM is O(n²) memory / O(n³) time, roughly 3× less memory and ~5× 

189 # faster — while the screen still acts as a conductor, so the matched 

190 # impedance stays in spec (≈ 57 + 14j vs the default's 60 + 8.5j) and the 

191 # bare radiator is unchanged (≈ 1.6 + 33j). Only the passive ground plane is 

192 # coarsened; the active feed/cage sleeve keeps its full resolution. 

193 coarse_params = MappingProxyType({"grid_pitch": 1.2192 / 17})

195 def build_wires(self): 

196 h = self.height 

197 pitch = self.grid_pitch 

198 n = max(2, round(self.plane_radius / pitch)) # plane radius, in cells 

199 s = 2 # fine cells per coarse cell — fine mesh at grid_pitch / 2 

200 r = max(1e-6, self.cage_radius) 

201 spacing = self.cage_ring_spacing 

202 base = spacing # cage bottom ring / feed segment: one ring-spacing up 

203 l1 = self.whip_lower_len 

204 m_cage = max(3, round(self.num_cage_wires)) 

205 n_posts = max(0, round(self.num_posts)) 

206 n_spokes = max(0, round(self.num_spokes)) 

207 

208 wires = [] 

209 

210 def add(p0, p1, nseg, ex=None, name=None): 

211 wires.append((p0, p1, nseg, ex, name) if name else (p0, p1, nseg, ex)) 

212 

213 # Unit direction of each cage wire. Snap the on-axis components to 

214 # exact zeros: wires are recognised as connected only where endpoint 

215 # coordinates match bitwise, and the post feet must land exactly on 

216 # the split points of the centre mesh lines below. 

217 dirs = [] 

218 for m in range(m_cage): 

219 th = 2.0 * math.pi * m / m_cage 

220 c, si = math.cos(th), math.sin(th) 

221 dirs.append((0.0 if abs(c) < 1e-15 else c, 0.0 if abs(si) < 1e-15 else si)) 

222 

223 def cage_pt(m, z): 

224 c, si = dirs[m % m_cage] 

225 return (r * c, r * si, z + h) 

226 

227 # --- whip: driven on its bottom segment, as the deck's EX card 

228 # (via the network's Driven port — see build_network) --- 

229 add((0.0, 0.0, h), (0.0, 0.0, base + h), 1, name="feed") 

230 add( 

231 (0.0, 0.0, base + h), 

232 (0.0, 0.0, l1 + h), 

233 max(1, round((l1 - base) / spacing)), 

234 ) 

235 # The thick top section keeps the deck's own 0.035" radius as a 

236 # per-wire spec; every other wire falls back to `wire_radius` via 

237 # build_wire_material (issue #388). 

238 wires.append( 

239 Wire( 

240 (0.0, 0.0, l1 + h), 

241 (0.0, 0.0, l1 + self.whip_upper_len + h), 

242 self.segs_for(self.whip_upper_len, _WHIP_UPPER_REF), 

243 spec=WireSpec(radius=self.whip_upper_radius), 

244 ) 

245 ) 

246 

247 # --- cage: verticals in ring-to-ring pieces, a polygon per ring --- 

248 n_levels = max(1, int((l1 - base) / spacing + 1e-9) + 1) 

249 levels = [base + l * spacing for l in range(n_levels)] 

250 for m in range(m_cage): 

251 for za, zb in zip(levels, levels[1:]): 

252 add(cage_pt(m, za), cage_pt(m, zb), 1) 

253 for z in levels: 

254 for m in range(m_cage): 

255 add(cage_pt(m, z), cage_pt(m + 1, z), 1) 

256 

257 post_idx = sorted( 

258 {round(k * m_cage / n_posts) % m_cage for k in range(n_posts)} 

259 ) 

260 spoke_idx = sorted( 

261 {round(k * m_cage / n_spokes) % m_cage for k in range(n_spokes)} 

262 ) 

263 # grounded posts: mesh up to the first ring. Named so build_network 

264 # can insert the deck's LD elements in series (post0 gets the coil, 

265 # post1 the capacitor — with the deck's 2 posts that is +x and −x). 

266 for k, m in enumerate(post_idx): 

267 c, si = dirs[m] 

268 add((r * c, r * si, h), cage_pt(m, base), 1, name=f"post{k}") 

269 for m in spoke_idx: # spokes: whip out to the first ring 

270 add((0.0, 0.0, base + h), cage_pt(m, base), 1) 

271 

272 # --- coarse ground grid, as unit cell edges so every crossing is a 

273 # wire-endpoint junction; the centre row/column and their +/-1 

274 # neighbours carry the deck's finer segmentation --- 

275 ecol = [_column_extent(i, n) for i in range(n + 1)] 

276 

277 def row_extent(j): 

278 # transpose of the column profile: the row at |j| reaches the 

279 # outermost column that reaches |j| 

280 return max((i for i in range(n + 1) if ecol[i] >= j), default=0) 

281 

282 for i in range(-n, n + 1): 

283 x = i * pitch 

284 dense = abs(i) <= 1 

285 e = ecol[abs(i)] 

286 for j in range(-e, e): 

287 if dense and j in (-1, 0): 

288 continue # covered by the fine centre mesh below 

289 add((x, j * pitch, h), (x, (j + 1) * pitch, h), s if dense else 1) 

290 for j in range(-n, n + 1): 

291 y = j * pitch 

292 dense = abs(j) <= 1 

293 e = row_extent(abs(j)) 

294 for i in range(-e, e): 

295 if dense and i in (-1, 0): 

296 continue 

297 add((i * pitch, y, h), ((i + 1) * pitch, y, h), s if dense else 1) 

298 

299 # --- fine centre mesh: the central +/-1-cell square at fine pitch --- 

300 def fine(k): 

301 # k-th fine node; the +/-s rim is written as +/-pitch itself so 

302 # the fine mesh and the coarse grid share exact coordinates 

303 return math.copysign(pitch, k) if abs(k) == s else k * pitch / s 

304 

305 x_feet = sorted({r * dirs[m][0] for m in post_idx if dirs[m][1] == 0.0}) 

306 y_feet = sorted({r * dirs[m][1] for m in post_idx if dirs[m][0] == 0.0}) 

307 

308 def line_pieces(feet): 

309 """Split points and per-piece segment counts for a centre mesh 

310 line that hosts post feet: break at each foot, and keep the whole 

311 line at ~foot-offset-length segments, as the deck's feed row.""" 

312 pts = [fine(k) for k in range(-s, s + 1)] 

313 pts += [ 

314 ft 

315 for ft in feet 

316 if -pitch < ft < pitch and not any(abs(ft - p) < 1e-12 for p in pts) 

317 ] 

318 pts.sort() 

319 seg = min(r, pitch / s) 

320 return [(a, b, max(1, round((b - a) / seg))) for a, b in zip(pts, pts[1:])] 

321 

322 for u in range(-s, s + 1): 

323 x = fine(u) 

324 if u == 0 and y_feet: 

325 for a, b, nseg in line_pieces(y_feet): 

326 add((x, a, h), (x, b, h), nseg) 

327 else: 

328 for v in range(-s, s): 

329 add((x, fine(v), h), (x, fine(v + 1), h), 1) 

330 for v in range(-s, s + 1): 

331 y = fine(v) 

332 if v == 0 and x_feet: 

333 for a, b, nseg in line_pieces(x_feet): 

334 add((a, y, h), (b, y, h), nseg) 

335 else: 

336 for u in range(-s, s): 

337 add((fine(u), y, h), (fine(u + 1), y, h), 1) 

338 

339 return wires 

340 

341 def build_network(self): 

342 """The deck's matching network: LD cards put 90 nH in series with 

343 the +x grounded post and 3 pF in series with the −x one (ld_card 

344 type 0 → `Load`, a series element in the named 1-segment post wire). 

345 A zero knob omits that element, leaving the post a plain wire; the 

346 feed is the whip's bottom segment, as the deck's EX card.""" 

347 ports = {"feed": PortOnWire("feed")} 

348 branches = [] 

349 n_posts = max(0, round(self.num_posts)) 

350 elems = [ 

351 ("post0", self.post_l_nh * 1e-9, None), 

352 ("post1", None, self.post_c_pf * 1e-12), 

353 ] 

354 for k, (pname, l, c) in enumerate(elems): 

355 if k >= n_posts or not (l or c): 

356 continue 

357 ports[pname] = PortOnWire(pname) 

358 branches.append(Load(port=pname, l=l or None, c=c or None)) 

359 return Network( 

360 ports=ports, 

361 branches=branches, 

362 sources=[Driven(port="feed", voltage=1 + 0j)], 

363 ) 

364 

365 def build_wire_material(self): 

366 return WireSpec(radius=self.wire_radius)
