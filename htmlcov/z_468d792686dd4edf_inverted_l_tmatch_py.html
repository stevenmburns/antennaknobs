1r"""10 m inverted-L worked on the 12 m band through a T-network tuner — 

2the first design with a pure interior circuit node (series C, shunt L, 

3series C), exercising the MNA network core on the classic "wire antenna + 

4T-match" situation. 

5 

6Geometry is inherited wholesale from `inverted_l`: the same 10 m 

7(28.57 MHz) bent, top-loaded vertical with four elevated radials. Operated 

8on 24.9 MHz that riser is electrically short and the feed is the textbook 

9tuner case — Z_ant ≈ 10.8 − 121.7j Ω (low R, big capacitive X), nowhere 

10near 50 Ω. 

11 

12A **T-network** (the standard ham "T-tuner" topology, a high-pass tee) 

13fixes it with two series capacitors flanking a shunt inductor: 

14 

15 source ──[ C1 series ]──┬──[ C2 series ]── feed 

16 │ 

17 [ L shunt ]── common 

18 

19The tuner is the station-stdlib `t_network_tuner` composite instanced as 

20``"tuner"`` between a virtual `in` node and the feed (issue #489); its tee 

21midpoint expands to the instance-internal node ``tuner.m`` — a pure 

22interior circuit node with no antenna segment, no TL, nothing but lumped 

23Group-2 branches meeting at a KCL row, which no other design exercises. 

24The stock coil has Q = 200 (a good air-wound coil; `coil_q = 0` recovers 

25the ideal one), and the stock element values are tuned WITH that coil 

26loss against the workbench's default solve: the design opens at ~50 Ω / 

27SWR ≈ 1.0 at 24.9 MHz, burning ~9 % of the input power in the coil — the 

28classic hidden T-tuner cost, visible in the power-budget readout. (The 

29sinusoidal reference basis reads the same tune at SWR ≈ 1.4 — the ~2 kΩ 

30virtual-resistance ride magnifies even basis-level differences in the 

31bare antenna Z; see the off-band guide on the site.) 

32 

33Because the antenna is short (R ≈ 11 Ω) the match must ride a virtual 

34resistance of ~2 kΩ, so there is no symmetric-capacitor solution and the 

35loaded Q is high (~13) — the real-world "narrow retune on a short 

36antenna" behavior, visible here as a sharp SWR notch in a frequency sweep. 

37 

38Degenerate slider endpoints (all legal since the MNA core, issue #285): 

39a 0 H shunt inductor hard-shorts the midpoint to common (input becomes 

40pure C1 reactance); a 0 F series capacitor is an OPEN — note this is the 

41opposite convention from the L-match's arms, where zero meant "element 

42absent": an absent series capacitor in a T-network is C → ∞ (a wire), not 

43C → 0. Setting `series_c2_pF = 0` disconnects the antenna entirely (the 

44source then sees only C1 + L), and `series_c1_pF = 0` open-circuits the 

45source itself. 

46"""

111 def build_wires(self): 

112 # Reuse the inverted-L geometry verbatim; rename the driven base gap 

113 # as the network's "feed" port and clear its inline excitation — the 

114 # T-network supplies the source at the virtual `in` node instead. 

115 return [ 

116 w._replace(ex=None, name="feed") if w.ex is not None else w 

117 for w in map(as_wire, super().build_wires())
