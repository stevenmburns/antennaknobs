1"""POTA wire-gauge tradeoff: a 20 m inverted-V where the wire is a knob. 

2 

3The park-activation question, as numbers (issue #318): a half-wave 

4inverted-V on a 7 m telescoping pole, with the **wire itself** selectable 

5from the `WIRES` catalog — 28/22/18 AWG, bare or PVC-insulated. Flip the 

6`wire_type` dropdown and watch three things move at once: 

7 

8* **weight** — the Info pane reports the total wire run and its grams 

9 (jacket included). 28 AWG is a tenth the copper of 18 AWG. 

10* **radiated power** — skin-effect loss (momwire#131 distributed loading; 

11 PyNEC's native LD 5) shows up as a *wire loss (I²R)* row in the power 

12 budget and as lost tenths of a dB. Thin wire pays roughly −0.3 dB on 

13 this antenna; thick wire nearly nothing. 

14* **SWR bandwidth** — two stacked effects the sweep untangles honestly: 

15 thicker wire widens the resonance *geometrically* (lower ln(L/a) Q), 

16 while thinner wire widens it *dissipatively* — bandwidth you pay for 

17 in watts. The band-locked sweep shows 20 m edge-to-edge. 

18 

19The PVC variants also land visibly lower in frequency than bare copper 

20cut to the same length — the insulated-wire velocity factor (a few 

21percent, King's jacket inductance), i.e. why a cut-to-formula insulated 

22dipole tunes long. The default `length_factor` is tuned so the stock 

2322 AWG PVC wire resonates near 14.1 MHz at 7 m over average ground; 

24switching to bare wire moves resonance UP — retune with the length knob 

25and note how much shorter the insulated antenna is. 

26 

27Geometry is the stock `dipoles.invvee` V (same knobs) at 20 m scale. 

28Both engines model the full wire: momwire's distributed loading, and 

29PyNEC via its native LD 5 (conductor loss) + LD 2 (jacket inductance, 

30issue #328) cards — engine-switching holds the story steady. 

31""" 

32 

33from types import MappingProxyType 

34 

35from antennaknobs.network import WIRES 

36from antennaknobs.designs.dipoles.invvee import Builder as InvVee 

37 

38 

39class Builder(InvVee): 

40 default_params = MappingProxyType( 

41 { 

42 **InvVee.default_params, 

43 # 20 m: the bread-and-butter POTA band. 

44 "design_freq": 14.1, 

45 "freq": 14.1, 

46 # Apex on a 7 m telescoping pole (≈0.33λ up on 20 m). 

47 "base": 7.0, 

48 # Tuned for the DEFAULT wire below (22 AWG PVC) to resonate 

49 # near 14.1 MHz at this height over average ground — insulated 

50 # wire is a few percent electrically longer than bare, so this 

51 # sits below the bare-wire invvee factor. 

52 "length_factor": 0.9440, 

53 "wire_type": "22-awg-pvc", 

54 "ui_params": MappingProxyType(

56 **InvVee.default_params["ui_params"], 

57 "wire_type": {"enum_options": tuple(sorted(WIRES))}, 

58 # The story lives inside the band: lock the sweep to the 

59 # band being measured so the SWR-bandwidth comparison 

60 # reads edge-to-edge on 20 m. 

61 "sweep_policy": {"anchor": "meas_freq", "band_locked": True}, 

62 }

64 }
